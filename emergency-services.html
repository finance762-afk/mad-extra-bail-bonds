Emergencies We Handle

Fast Response for Critical Plumbing Situations

When a plumbing disaster strikes your home or business, Precision Plumbing Solutions is the team to call. We respond quickly to all types of emergencies across the greater Boston area.

Burst Pipe Repair

A burst pipe can release hundreds of gallons of water per hour, causing catastrophic damage to your walls, floors, and belongings. Shut off your main water valve immediately and call us. We arrive fast, stop the flooding, and repair or replace the damaged pipe to get your home back to normal.

Sewer Backup

Sewage backing up into your home is a health hazard that requires immediate professional attention. Stop using all water fixtures and call us right away. We clear blockages, clean sewer lines, and restore proper flow to protect your family's health and your property.

Water Heater Failure

A leaking or failed water heater can flood your basement and leave your family without hot water. Turn off the power supply and water inlet valve, then call us. We diagnose the problem quickly and provide same-day repair or emergency replacement to restore your hot water.

Gas Leak Response

If you smell rotten eggs or hear hissing near a gas line, this is a life-threatening emergency. Leave the building immediately, do not flip any switches, and call 911 first, then call us. Our licensed plumbers locate and repair gas leaks safely to protect your family and property.

Flooding & Water Damage Prevention

Active flooding from any plumbing source demands immediate action to minimize damage. Shut off the main water supply and move valuables to higher ground. We respond rapidly to stop the source, remove standing water risk, and make repairs to prevent further damage to your home.

Frozen Pipe Emergencies

Massachusetts winters can freeze pipes quickly, and frozen pipes are one burst away from flooding your home. Do not use a torch or open flame — call us immediately. We safely thaw frozen pipes and repair any damage before it escalates into a major water emergency.

Be Prepared

What to Do While You Wait for Your Emergency Plumber

Taking quick action before we arrive can save you thousands of dollars in damage. Follow these steps depending on your situation.

Shut Off the Water

Locate your main water shut-off valve and turn it off immediately. This is the single most important step to prevent further flooding and water damage. If you don't know where your shut-off valve is, we can guide you over the phone while we're on our way.

Turn Off Electricity

If water is near electrical outlets, appliances, or your breaker panel, shut off the electricity to affected areas from your circuit breaker. Standing water and electricity are a deadly combination. Never step into standing water near live electrical sources.

Document the Damage

Take photos and videos of the damage for your insurance claim. Document the source of the leak, the extent of water damage, and any damaged belongings. This documentation will be invaluable when filing your insurance claim later.

Protect Your Belongings

Move furniture, electronics, and valuables away from the affected area. Place towels around the source if possible to contain spreading water. If the leak is from above, place buckets underneath to catch dripping water and minimize floor damage.

For Gas Leaks: Get Out

If you smell gas, leave the building immediately. Do not turn on or off any light switches or electrical devices. Do not use your phone inside the building. Once you're safely outside, call 911 first, then call us at (978) 317-1244.

Stay Calm — Help Is Coming

We know a plumbing emergency is stressful. Once you've called us and taken the immediate safety steps above, know that help is on the way. Our experienced plumbers have handled thousands of emergencies and will take care of the situation.
