Tank vs. Tankless Water Heaters

Choosing the right water heater for your home is an important decision that affects your comfort, energy bills, and available space. Here is what you need to know about each type:

Traditional Tank Water Heaters

Tank water heaters store a reservoir of hot water -- typically 40 to 80 gallons -- that is ready for use whenever you need it. They are the most common type of water heater found in Massachusetts homes and offer several advantages:

  • Lower upfront cost compared to tankless systems
  • Simple, proven technology with reliable performance
  • Works well for homes with moderate hot water demand
  • Available in electric, natural gas, and propane models
  • Easier and less expensive to install in most homes

Tankless Water Heaters

Tankless (on-demand) water heaters heat water only when you need it, eliminating the energy waste of keeping a tank hot 24/7. They are an increasingly popular choice for homeowners looking to save on energy costs:

  • Up to 30% more energy efficient than traditional tank systems
  • Endless hot water supply -- never run out during back-to-back showers
  • Compact, wall-mounted design saves valuable floor space
  • Longer lifespan -- typically 20+ years compared to 10-15 for tank models
  • Reduced risk of water damage from tank leaks or failures

Water Heater Repair

Not every water heater problem requires a full replacement. Many issues can be resolved with a professional repair that extends the life of your existing system. We commonly repair:

  • No hot water or insufficient hot water
  • Pilot light that will not stay lit
  • Strange rumbling, popping, or banging noises
  • Leaking from the tank, fittings, or pressure relief valve
  • Rusty or discolored hot water
  • Fluctuating water temperature
  • Thermostat and heating element failures
  • Anode rod replacement to prevent tank corrosion

When to repair vs. replace: As a general rule, if your water heater is over 10 years old and needs a major repair, replacement is usually the more cost-effective option. We will always give you an honest assessment and help you make the best decision for your situation and budget.

Signs Your Water Heater Needs Attention

Recognizing the early warning signs of water heater trouble can prevent a complete failure and potential water damage. Call Precision Plumbing Solutions if you notice:

  • It takes significantly longer than usual for water to get hot
  • Hot water runs out much faster than it used to
  • You see water pooling around the base of your water heater
  • Your hot water has a metallic taste or rusty appearance
  • The unit is making unusual sounds during heating cycles
  • Your energy bills have increased without a change in usage
  • The water heater is over 10 years old and has not been serviced

Water Heater Maintenance

Regular maintenance extends the life of your water heater and keeps it running at peak efficiency. We recommend annual maintenance that includes flushing sediment from the tank, inspecting the anode rod, checking the pressure relief valve, and verifying all connections are secure and leak-free. A well-maintained water heater can last years longer than a neglected one, saving you money in the long run.
