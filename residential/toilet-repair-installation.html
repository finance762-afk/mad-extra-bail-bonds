Whether it is a toilet that will not stop running, a persistent leak at the base, or an aging unit that needs to be replaced entirely, Precision Plumbing Solutions provides fast, reliable toilet repair and installation services throughout Lynnfield, MA and the surrounding communities. Common Toilet Repairs We Handle

Our experienced plumbers diagnose and repair every type of toilet problem. Here are the most common issues we see in homes across Essex, Middlesex, Suffolk, Norfolk, and Plymouth counties:

Running Toilets

A toilet that runs constantly or intermittently is usually caused by a worn flapper valve, a faulty fill valve, or an improperly adjusted float. While it may seem like a minor annoyance, a running toilet can waste up to 200 gallons of water per day. Our technicians quickly identify the root cause and replace the defective components to stop the waste and restore quiet, efficient operation.

Leaking Toilet Base

Water pooling around the base of your toilet is a sign that the wax ring seal between the toilet and the floor flange has failed. This is not just a cosmetic issue -- sewage gases and wastewater can seep through the broken seal, creating health hazards and damaging your flooring. We carefully remove the toilet, replace the wax ring, inspect the flange for damage, and reset the toilet with a proper, watertight seal.

Weak or Incomplete Flush

If your toilet is not flushing with full force or requires multiple flushes to clear the bowl, the problem could stem from clogged rim jets, a low water level in the tank, a worn flapper that closes too quickly, or mineral buildup restricting water flow. We thoroughly inspect the flush mechanism and internal passages to restore full flushing power.

Phantom Flushes

A phantom flush occurs when your toilet spontaneously refills as if it has been flushed, even when no one has used it. This is typically caused by a slow leak from the tank into the bowl through a deteriorated flapper. We replace the flapper and inspect the flush valve seat to eliminate the phantom flushing and prevent water waste.

Toilet Clogs

Stubborn toilet clogs that resist a standard plunger may indicate a deeper blockage in the drain line or an obstruction lodged in the toilet trap. We use professional-grade augers and diagnostic tools to clear even the most persistent clogs without damaging your toilet or drain pipes.

Wax Ring & Flange Repair

The wax ring and toilet flange are critical components that create a watertight seal between your toilet and the drain pipe. Over time, wax rings dry out, crack, and lose their sealing ability. Flanges can corrode, crack, or become loose from the subfloor. When either component fails, you may notice water leaking at the base, unpleasant sewer odors, or a toilet that rocks when you sit on it. Our plumbers expertly replace wax rings and repair or replace damaged flanges to restore a solid, leak-free connection.

Do not ignore a rocking toilet. A toilet that wobbles or rocks when you sit on it may have a broken flange, deteriorated wax ring, or uneven floor. Continuing to use a rocking toilet will worsen the damage and can lead to a complete seal failure, resulting in water damage and sewage leaks. Call us at the first sign of movement.

Toilet Valve Replacement

The fill valve and flush valve inside your toilet tank are the mechanical heart of the system. The fill valve controls how water enters the tank after a flush, while the flush valve releases the water into the bowl. When these components wear out, you may experience running water, incomplete fills, slow tank refilling, or weak flushes. We replace worn valves with high-quality, durable replacements that restore proper function and extend the life of your toilet.

Signs You Need Toilet Repair or Replacement

Knowing when to repair and when to replace your toilet can save you money in the long run. Contact Precision Plumbing Solutions if you notice any of these warning signs:

  • Toilet runs constantly or cycles on and off by itself
  • Water leaking around the base of the toilet
  • Cracks visible in the porcelain tank or bowl
  • Frequent clogs that require plunging multiple times per week
  • Toilet wobbles or rocks on the floor
  • Persistent sewer odors in the bathroom
  • High water bills from an inefficient older toilet
  • Toilet requires multiple flushes to clear the bowl
