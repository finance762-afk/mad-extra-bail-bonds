Professional Pipe Repair & Replacement in Lynnfield, MA

Your home's plumbing pipes are the backbone of the entire system, carrying fresh water to every fixture and removing wastewater safely. When pipes deteriorate, leak, or burst, the consequences can range from minor water stains to catastrophic flooding that damages walls, ceilings, floors, and personal belongings. Pipe Repair Services

Not every pipe problem requires a full replacement. In many cases, targeted repairs can restore the integrity of your plumbing system and extend its lifespan. Here are the most common pipe repair issues we handle:

Leaking Pipes

Pipe leaks can develop at joints, fittings, or anywhere along the pipe itself. Even a small drip can waste thousands of gallons of water per year and create an environment for mold and mildew growth behind your walls. We locate the source of every leak using advanced detection methods and perform precise repairs that eliminate the problem without unnecessary disruption to your home.

Burst Pipes

A burst pipe is a plumbing emergency that demands immediate response. Whether caused by freezing temperatures, excessive water pressure, corrosion, or physical damage, a burst pipe can release hundreds of gallons of water per hour into your home. Our 24/7 emergency team responds quickly to shut off the water supply, mitigate damage, and perform the necessary repairs or replacements to get your system back online.

Corroded Pipes

Corrosion is a gradual process that weakens pipe walls from the inside out. Galvanized steel pipes are especially prone to internal corrosion and rust buildup, which restricts water flow and eventually leads to leaks and failures. Copper pipes can also develop corrosion, particularly in areas with aggressive water chemistry. We assess the extent of corrosion and recommend either targeted repairs or full replacement depending on the condition of your system.

Pinhole Leaks

Pinhole leaks are tiny perforations that develop in copper pipes due to pitting corrosion caused by water chemistry imbalances. These leaks are often difficult to detect because they can occur behind walls and under floors, causing hidden water damage for weeks or months before becoming noticeable. Our technicians are experienced at locating and repairing pinhole leaks, and we can advise you on whether isolated repairs or a system-wide solution is the best approach.

Pipe Replacement & Repiping Services

When repairs are no longer cost-effective or your piping system has reached the end of its useful life, a full or partial repipe is the smart long-term investment. We specialize in the following pipe replacement services:

  • Copper repiping: The gold standard in residential plumbing -- durable, corrosion-resistant, and proven to last 50 years or more
  • PEX repiping: A flexible, cost-effective alternative to copper that resists freezing, requires fewer fittings, and installs faster with less disruption to your walls
  • PVC and CPVC pipes: Ideal for drain lines and certain supply applications, offering excellent corrosion resistance at a lower cost
  • Galvanized pipe replacement: Removing old, corroded galvanized steel pipes and upgrading to copper or PEX for improved water pressure and quality
  • Cast iron pipe replacement: Replacing deteriorated cast iron drain and sewer pipes with modern PVC or ABS alternatives

Pipe Materials Comparison

Choosing the right pipe material for your home depends on your budget, the application, and your long-term goals. Here is how the most common residential pipe materials compare:

Copper is the most trusted material for water supply lines. It resists corrosion, handles high temperatures, and has a proven track record spanning decades. Copper is more expensive upfront but delivers outstanding longevity and reliability. It is also fully recyclable.

PEX (cross-linked polyethylene) has become the most popular choice for modern repiping projects. It is flexible, freeze-resistant, and significantly less expensive than copper. PEX can be routed through walls with minimal cutting and requires fewer connections, which means fewer potential leak points. It is available in color-coded red and blue for easy hot and cold identification.

Galvanized steel was commonly used in homes built before the 1960s. While durable initially, galvanized pipes corrode from the inside over time, leading to restricted water flow, discolored water, and eventual leaks. If your home still has galvanized pipes, we strongly recommend upgrading to copper or PEX before problems escalate.

When to Repair vs. Replace Your Pipes

Deciding between a repair and a full replacement depends on several factors. Here are some guidelines to help you understand when each option makes sense:

  • Repair when the issue is isolated to one or two locations and the rest of the system is in good condition
  • Repair when your pipes are relatively new and made of durable materials like copper or PEX
  • Replace when you are experiencing recurring leaks in multiple locations throughout the house
  • Replace when your home has galvanized steel pipes that are over 40 years old
  • Replace when you notice discolored or rusty water coming from your faucets
  • Replace when water pressure has been steadily declining despite no issues with the municipal supply
  • Replace when the cost of frequent repairs is approaching the cost of a repipe

Pipe Insulation Services

Massachusetts winters bring freezing temperatures that pose a serious threat to unprotected pipes. Pipes in unheated areas such as basements, crawl spaces, attics, and exterior walls are particularly vulnerable to freezing and bursting. Precision Plumbing Solutions offers professional pipe insulation services to protect your plumbing system from cold weather damage, reduce heat loss from hot water pipes, and prevent condensation on cold water lines during humid summer months.
