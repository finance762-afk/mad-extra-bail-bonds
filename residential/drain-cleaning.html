Types of Drain Clogs We Handle

Different drains face different challenges. Here is a breakdown of the most common drain issues we encounter and resolve for homeowners across the greater Boston area:

Kitchen Drain Clogs

Kitchen drains are among the most frequently clogged drains in any home. Grease, cooking oils, food particles, and soap residue gradually build up along the interior walls of your pipes, narrowing the passage and eventually causing a complete blockage. We use specialized snaking and hydro-jetting equipment to cut through grease buildup and restore your kitchen drain to full capacity.

Bathroom Drain Clogs

Hair, soap scum, toothpaste, and personal care products are the primary culprits behind bathroom drain clogs. These materials combine to form thick, stubborn blockages that standard plungers often cannot clear. Our drain cleaning professionals use the right tools for the job, whether it is a bathroom sink, shower drain, or bathtub drain that needs attention.

Main Sewer Line Clogs

When multiple drains in your home are backing up simultaneously, the problem likely lies in your main sewer line. Tree root intrusion, accumulated debris, and pipe deterioration are common causes of main line clogs. We use video camera inspection to diagnose the exact location and nature of the blockage before performing targeted cleaning to clear the line effectively.

Our Drain Cleaning Methods

We employ a range of professional drain cleaning techniques to match the specific type and severity of your clog:

  • Drain Snaking: A motorized auger that physically breaks through clogs and pulls debris out of the pipe
  • Hydro-Jetting: High-pressure water jets that scour the interior walls of pipes, removing grease, scale, and buildup
  • Video Camera Inspection: A fiber-optic camera inserted into the drain to visually identify the location and cause of blockages
  • Drain Line Locating: Electronic equipment to pinpoint the exact location of clogs in buried or hidden pipes
  • Preventive Maintenance: Scheduled cleaning to prevent clogs before they happen, ideal for older homes and commercial properties

Signs You Need Professional Drain Cleaning

Catching drain problems early can save you from costly repairs and water damage. Contact Precision Plumbing Solutions if you notice any of these warning signs:

  • Water draining slowly from sinks, showers, or bathtubs
  • Gurgling sounds coming from drains when water is running
  • Unpleasant odors rising from drains
  • Water backing up in sinks or tubs when you flush the toilet
  • Standing water in your shower or around floor drains
  • Multiple drains clogging at the same time
  • Fruit flies or drain flies appearing near sinks

Do not use chemical drain cleaners. Store-bought chemical drain cleaners may seem like a quick fix, but they can corrode your pipes over time and often only partially clear clogs, leading to recurring problems. Professional drain cleaning is safer for your plumbing and far more effective.
