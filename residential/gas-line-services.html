Gas Line Services

Licensed gas line installation, repair, and leak detection for homes throughout Lynnfield, MA and the greater Boston area.

Gas Safety Warning — Licensed Professionals Only

Gas line work is not a DIY project. Improper gas line installation, repair, or modification can result in gas leaks, carbon monoxide poisoning, explosions, and death. Massachusetts state law requires that all gas line work be performed by a licensed professional. Precision Plumbing Solutions is fully licensed and insured for all gas line services. Never attempt to repair, modify, or install gas lines yourself. If you smell gas, leave the area immediately, call 911, and then call us.

Gas Line Installation

Whether you are building a new home, adding a gas appliance, or extending your existing gas supply to a new area of the house, Precision Plumbing Solutions handles every aspect of gas line installation with precision and care.

New Construction Gas Line Installation

We work with homeowners and builders to design and install complete gas piping systems for new construction projects. This includes sizing the gas supply lines correctly for the total BTU demand of all planned appliances, running lines through walls and floors with proper support and protection, and ensuring every connection is leak-tested and code-compliant before the system goes live. Proper sizing is critical -- undersized gas lines can cause appliances to underperform and create safety hazards.

Gas Appliance Hookups

Adding a new gas appliance to your home requires more than just connecting a flexible line. We ensure the gas supply to the location is properly sized for the appliance's BTU requirements, install the appropriate shut-off valve, connect the appliance with code-approved connectors and fittings, and test every connection for leaks. We handle hookups for all types of gas appliances:

  • Gas stoves and ranges
  • Gas dryers
  • Gas water heaters (tank and tankless)
  • Gas fireplaces and fireplace inserts
  • Outdoor gas grills and built-in BBQ islands
  • Gas fire pits and patio heaters
  • Gas furnaces and boilers
  • Gas pool and spa heaters

Need a new boiler installed? Gas-fired boilers are one of the most common heating systems in Massachusetts homes. Gas Line Repair

Gas line problems demand immediate professional attention. Corroded fittings, damaged pipes, loose connections, and aging materials can all lead to gas leaks that put your family at risk. Our licensed technicians diagnose gas line issues quickly and perform lasting repairs using code-approved materials and techniques. We repair gas lines of all types, including black iron pipe, corrugated stainless steel tubing (CSST), and copper, and we replace outdated or deteriorating sections before they become dangerous.

Gas Leak Detection and Repair

A gas leak is a life-threatening emergency that requires immediate professional response. Our gas leak detection services use specialized electronic gas detectors and pressure testing equipment to locate leaks with pinpoint accuracy -- even small leaks that may not produce an obvious odor. Once we identify the source, we perform the repair immediately and retest the entire system to confirm it is sealed and safe.

Signs of a Gas Leak

Knowing the warning signs of a gas leak can save lives. Contact us immediately or call 911 if you notice any of the following:

  • Rotten egg or sulfur smell: Natural gas is odorless, but utility companies add mercaptan to give it a distinctive smell for safety
  • Hissing or whistling sound: A hissing noise near a gas line or appliance indicates gas escaping under pressure
  • Dead or dying vegetation: Plants dying in an unusual pattern near a buried gas line can indicate an underground leak
  • Unexpectedly high gas bills: A sudden spike in your gas bill without increased usage may mean gas is escaping somewhere in the system
  • Bubbles in standing water: Gas leaking from an underground line can cause bubbling in puddles or wet areas
  • Physical symptoms: Headaches, dizziness, nausea, or fatigue can be signs of gas exposure -- leave the area immediately

Gas Line Inspection and Testing

Regular gas line inspection and pressure testing is essential for maintaining the safety of your home's gas system. We recommend gas line inspections when purchasing a home, after any renovation work near gas lines, if your gas appliances are not performing correctly, or if your system is more than 20 years old. Our inspection process includes a visual examination of all accessible gas piping and connections, pressure testing the system to identify even the smallest leaks, checking all shut-off valves for proper operation, inspecting appliance connections and flexible connectors for wear, and verifying that all gas piping meets current Massachusetts code requirements.

Natural Gas vs. Propane

Many homes in the Lynnfield area and surrounding communities use natural gas supplied by the local utility, while some homes -- particularly in more rural areas -- rely on propane (LP gas) delivered to an on-site tank. While both fuels serve the same purpose, they have different properties that affect installation. Natural gas is lighter than air and rises when it leaks, while propane is heavier than air and settles in low areas, making propane leaks particularly dangerous in basements and crawl spaces. The two fuels also require different orifice sizes in appliances and different regulator pressures. Our licensed technicians are experienced with both natural gas and propane systems and can install, repair, and convert appliances between the two fuel types as needed.

Gas Line Safety Tips

Following these safety practices can help protect your family and home:

  • Know where your main gas shut-off valve is located and how to turn it off
  • Install carbon monoxide detectors on every level of your home and near all gas appliances
  • Never use gas appliances for purposes they were not designed for, such as using a gas oven to heat your home
  • Keep the area around gas appliances clear of combustible materials
  • Have your gas lines and appliances inspected annually by a licensed professional
  • Never ignore the smell of gas, even if it seems faint -- leave and call for help
  • Call 811 before digging anywhere on your property to have underground gas lines marked

Emergency Gas Leak Response

If you suspect a gas leak in your home, follow these steps immediately:

  • Do not turn on or off any electrical switches, lights, or appliances
  • Do not use your phone inside the house
  • Do not light matches, lighters, or candles
  • Open windows and doors if you can do so safely and quickly
  • Leave the house immediately and take everyone with you
  • Once safely outside, call 911 and your gas utility company
  • Call Precision Plumbing Solutions at (978) 317-1244 for emergency repair
