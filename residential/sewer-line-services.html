Sewer Line Services

Comprehensive sewer line inspection, repair, and replacement for homeowners throughout Lynnfield, MA and the greater Boston area.

Professional Sewer Line Services in Lynnfield, MA

Your sewer line is one of the most critical components of your home's plumbing system. It carries all wastewater from your drains, toilets, and appliances to the municipal sewer or your septic system. When something goes wrong with your sewer line, the consequences can be severe -- from slow drains and foul odors to raw sewage backing up into your home. At Precision Plumbing Solutions, we provide comprehensive sewer line services including inspection, cleaning, repair, and full replacement to keep your system functioning properly and protect your home from costly damage.

As a second generation plumbing company serving Lynnfield, MA and the surrounding communities, we have the experience and equipment to diagnose and resolve any sewer line issue quickly and effectively. We use the latest technology including video camera inspections and trenchless repair methods to minimize disruption to your property while delivering lasting results.

Video Camera Sewer Line Inspection

Accurate diagnosis is the first step in solving any sewer line problem. We use high-definition video camera inspection technology to see inside your sewer line in real time. A waterproof camera is fed through the pipe, transmitting a live video feed that allows us to identify the exact location and nature of any blockage, damage, or deterioration. This eliminates guesswork and ensures we recommend the most effective and cost-efficient solution for your specific situation.

Video camera inspections are invaluable for identifying issues such as tree root intrusion, pipe corrosion, bellied or sagging sections, joint separation, cracks, and buildup accumulation. We also recommend camera inspections for homebuyers who want to assess the condition of a property's sewer line before closing.

Sewer Line Repair

When your sewer line is damaged but still structurally sound in most areas, targeted repair is often the most practical and affordable option. Our team can repair cracked, broken, or offset pipe sections without replacing the entire line. We assess the severity and location of the damage through camera inspection and then determine the best repair approach -- whether that involves spot repair, pipe lining, or section replacement.

Trenchless Sewer Repair

Traditional sewer line repair often requires digging a trench across your yard, tearing up landscaping, driveways, and walkways in the process. Trenchless sewer repair eliminates most of this disruption. Using pipe lining or pipe bursting techniques, we can repair or replace your sewer line with minimal excavation. Pipe lining involves inserting a flexible, resin-coated liner into the existing pipe, which hardens to form a smooth, durable new pipe within the old one. Pipe bursting breaks apart the old pipe while simultaneously pulling a new pipe into place. Both methods are faster, less invasive, and often more cost-effective than traditional dig-and-replace methods.

Sewer Line Replacement

In some cases, a sewer line is too far gone for repair. Extensive corrosion, multiple breaks, collapsed sections, or severely deteriorated pipe material may require a full sewer line replacement. When replacement is necessary, we work with you to choose the best approach -- whether traditional excavation or trenchless pipe bursting -- based on your property, budget, and the condition of the existing line. We install high-quality, long-lasting pipe materials designed to provide decades of reliable service.

Tree Root Intrusion

Tree roots are one of the most common causes of sewer line damage in residential properties. Roots are naturally drawn to the moisture and nutrients inside sewer pipes, and they can penetrate even small cracks or joint gaps. Once inside, roots grow rapidly, catching debris and creating blockages that worsen over time. Left untreated, root intrusion can crack and eventually collapse your sewer pipe entirely. We use mechanical root cutting to clear existing intrusions and can recommend solutions to prevent future root damage, including root barriers and pipe lining.

Sewer Line Cleaning

Regular sewer line cleaning helps prevent blockages, backups, and premature pipe deterioration. Over time, grease, soap, mineral deposits, and organic matter accumulate along the interior walls of your sewer line, gradually restricting flow. We use professional hydro-jetting equipment that blasts high-pressure water through the pipe, scouring away buildup and restoring full flow capacity. Routine cleaning is especially important for older homes with cast iron or clay pipes that are more susceptible to buildup and root intrusion.

Signs of Sewer Line Problems

Sewer line issues often develop gradually, and catching the warning signs early can save you from extensive damage and costly emergency repairs. Contact Precision Plumbing Solutions if you notice any of the following:

  • Multiple drains in your home are slow or backing up simultaneously
  • Gurgling sounds coming from toilets or drains
  • Sewage or rotten egg smell inside your home or in your yard
  • Wet, soggy spots in your yard even during dry weather
  • Unusually lush or green patches of grass over the sewer line path
  • Foundation cracks or settling that appeared recently
  • Sewage backing up through basement floor drains
  • Pest problems -- rodents and insects are attracted to sewer line breaks

Do not ignore sewer line warning signs. A damaged sewer line will not fix itself, and problems only worsen with time. What starts as a minor blockage or slow drain can escalate into a full sewer backup, causing thousands of dollars in water damage and creating serious health hazards. Early inspection and repair are always more affordable than emergency replacement.

Preventive Sewer Line Maintenance

The best way to avoid expensive sewer line emergencies is through regular preventive maintenance. We recommend scheduling a video camera inspection every few years to monitor the condition of your sewer line and catch small problems before they become major issues. Periodic hydro-jetting keeps the line clear of buildup, and root treatment helps prevent intrusion in areas with heavy tree growth. Our team can set up a maintenance schedule tailored to the age and condition of your sewer system.
