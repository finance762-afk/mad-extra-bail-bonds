Backflow Testing & Prevention Services in Lynnfield, MA

Backflow is a serious plumbing issue that occurs when contaminated water flows backward into the clean water supply. This can happen when there is a sudden drop in water pressure, a burst water main, or a cross-connection between potable and non-potable water systems. The consequences of backflow contamination can be severe, potentially introducing chemicals, bacteria, pesticides, and other harmful substances into the drinking water supply for your building and even your neighbors.

At Precision Plumbing Solutions, we provide comprehensive backflow testing and prevention services for commercial and residential properties throughout Lynnfield, MA and the greater Boston area. Our licensed technicians are certified to test, install, repair, and maintain backflow prevention devices, ensuring your property remains compliant with Massachusetts regulations and your water supply stays safe.

What Is Backflow and Why Is Testing Required?

Backflow occurs through two primary mechanisms: back-siphonage and back-pressure. Back-siphonage happens when negative pressure in the water supply line draws contaminated water backward through a cross-connection. This can occur during fire hydrant use, water main breaks, or high water demand situations. Back-pressure occurs when the pressure in a non-potable system exceeds the pressure in the potable water supply, forcing contaminated water into the clean supply line.

Massachusetts state regulations require backflow prevention devices on commercial properties and any residential property with a potential cross-connection to a non-potable water source, such as irrigation systems, swimming pools, boilers, or fire suppression systems. These devices must be tested annually by a certified tester to verify they are functioning correctly. Failure to comply can result in fines, water service disconnection, and potential liability if contamination occurs.

Annual Backflow Testing

Annual backflow testing is a legal requirement for properties with installed backflow prevention devices. During a backflow test, our certified technician uses specialized gauges and equipment to verify that each component of your backflow preventer is operating within acceptable parameters. The test confirms that check valves are sealing properly, relief valves are opening at the correct differential pressure, and the overall assembly is preventing backflow as designed.

Our backflow testing service includes:

  • Complete testing of all backflow prevention assemblies on your property
  • Detailed test reports filed with your local water authority
  • Identification of any components that need repair or replacement
  • On-the-spot minor repairs when possible
  • Scheduling reminders so you never miss your annual testing deadline
  • Documentation for insurance and compliance records

We submit all required test reports directly to the appropriate water department on your behalf, saving you the administrative burden and ensuring timely compliance.

Backflow Preventer Installation

If your property requires a new backflow prevention device, whether for new construction, a renovation, or to bring an existing property into compliance, Precision Plumbing Solutions handles the complete installation process. We assess your property to determine the appropriate type and size of backflow preventer needed, obtain necessary permits, perform the installation to code, and schedule the initial certification test.

We install all types of backflow prevention assemblies, including reduced pressure zone (RPZ) devices, double check valve assemblies (DCVA), pressure vacuum breakers (PVB), and atmospheric vacuum breakers (AVB). The specific type required depends on the degree of hazard posed by the potential cross-connection and the requirements of your local water authority.

Residential Backflow Services

Backflow prevention is not just a commercial concern. Many residential properties in Lynnfield and surrounding communities also require backflow testing and prevention, particularly homes with irrigation systems, swimming pools, radiant heating systems, or private wells with a connection to the public water supply. If you have received a notice from your water department requiring backflow testing, or if you are installing a new irrigation system or other potential cross-connection, we can help you understand your requirements and get into compliance quickly and affordably.
